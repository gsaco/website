Working Paper

Finite-Sample Failures and Condition-Number Diagnostics in Double Machine Learning

Gabriel Saco

This paper investigates the finite-sample behavior of Double Machine Learning (DML) estimators, identifying conditions under which standard asymptotic approximations fail. I propose novel condition-number diagnostics to detect and address numerical instabilities in high-dimensional causal inference settings. The analysis shows that ill-conditioning in the first-stage estimation can lead to severely distorted inference even with large samples, and provides practical guidance for practitioners.

Working Paper

Economic Freedom Shocks: Global Evidence of Asymmetric and Nonlinear Effects

Gabriel Saco - Supervisor: Diego Winkelried (PhD Cambridge)

This paper estimates impulse-response functions of output to discrete economic freedom shocks using panel Local Projections (Jord√†, 2005). I construct cumulative shock measures from the EFW index and decompose them into positive (pro-market reform) and negative (institutional deterioration) episodes. The identification leverages country and year fixed effects, exploiting quasi-exogenous institutional variation. I allow asymmetric dynamics: positive and negative shocks differ in magnitude, speed and persistence, with effects depending on shock size and initial institutional conditions.
